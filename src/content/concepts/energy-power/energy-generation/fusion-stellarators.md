# Stellarators (Renaissance, Type One)

*Kind: technology*

## Physics / mechanism

Stellarators confine plasma in a twisted, non-axisymmetric magnetic field generated entirely by external coils — no net toroidal current required. The field topology is shaped via helical or modular coils to cancel neoclassical transport losses. Key parameters: energy confinement time (τ_E), plasma beta (ratio of plasma to magnetic pressure), and ion temperature. Wendelstein 7-X (W7-X, IPP Greifswald) is the current SoA: optimised for quasi-isodynamic configuration, achieved ~8 minutes of steady-state plasma at ~20 MW heating power, ion temperatures exceeding 40 million K, and confinement competitive with mid-scale tokamaks. No disruption risk — structurally the central differentiator versus tokamaks.

## Cloudberry relevance

Tokamaks dominate fusion funding (ITER, Commonwealth Fusion, TAE, Helion) but require active current drive and carry disruption risk. Inertial confinement (NIF, Focused Energy) is pulse-based. Compact stellarators — Type One Energy, Renaissance Fusion — pursue high-field, high-beta designs using HTS (high-temperature superconducting) coils and liquid lithium/FLiBe blankets.

| Approach | Steady-state | Disruption risk | Coil complexity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tokamak | No (pulsed) | High | Moderate |
| Stellarator | Yes | None | Very high |
| Inertial | No (pulsed) | N/A | Low (driver) |

---

Renaissance Fusion (Grenoble) sits squarely in Cloudberry's geography and thesis: advanced materials (HTS tape, plasma-facing composites), precision manufacturing at the limits of semiconductor-grade tolerances, and photonic/metrology tooling for coil QA. The GlobalFoundries-LP fund angle is indirect but real — HTS coil fabrication pulls on thin-film deposition and process control IP. Monitor Renaissance's Series A as a potential deal-flow entry.
