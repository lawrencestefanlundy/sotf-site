# GaAs Photonics

*Kind: technology*

## Physics / mechanism

GaAs is a III-V direct-bandgap semiconductor (Eg ≈ 1.42 eV at 300 K) with electron mobility ~8500 cm²/V·s—roughly 6× silicon. Direct bandgap enables efficient radiative recombination, making it the substrate of choice for near-IR lasers (750–900 nm), VCSELs, and high-efficiency photovoltaics. Epitaxial stacks grown by MBE or MOCVD achieve threshold current densities <200 A/cm² in edge-emitters; VCSEL wall-plug efficiencies reach ~60% at 850 nm. Multi-junction GaAs solar cells exceed 47% efficiency under concentration. RF-photonic integration exploits high-speed Schottky junctions and low parasitic capacitance. Wafer sizes have reached 6-inch production with 8-inch in qualification at select fabs.

## Competitive landscape

GaAs competes directly with InP (dominant at 1310/1550 nm for telecom), GaN (power, short-wavelength), and silicon photonics (cost, CMOS integration). Emerging GaN-on-Si threatens GaAs in some mm-wave + photonic co-integration scenarios.

## Cloudberry relevance

| Platform | Wavelength sweet spot | Integration density | Fab accessibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| GaAs | 750–980 nm | Medium | Limited (IQE, WIN, Landmark) |
| InP | 1300–1600 nm | High (monolithic) | Very limited |
| Si Photonics | 1100–2000 nm | Very high | Broad (GF, TSMC) |

Cost per wafer remains ~10× silicon; this constrains volume consumer plays but protects margin in defense, LiDAR, and datacom VCSEL markets.


GaAs VCSEL supply chains are consolidating—LiDAR and 3D sensing (iPhone, autonomous vehicles) are primary volume drivers. Deal-flow angles: fabless GaAs design houses needing captive capacity, GaAs-on-silicon heterogeneous integration startups, and VCSEL array suppliers for datacom. GlobalFoundries' lack of III-V capability creates a structural gap; portfolio companies bridging GaAs epitaxy to CMOS backend are directly relevant. Lunar Ventures' deep-tech remit makes early-stage GaAs photonic IC plays natural co-investment targets.
