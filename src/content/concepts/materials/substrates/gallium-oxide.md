# Gallium Oxide (Ga2O3)

*Kind: material*

## Physics / mechanism

Gallium oxide (β-Ga₂O₃) is an ultrawide-bandgap (UWBG) semiconductor, Eg ≈ 4.8 eV, yielding a theoretical breakdown field ~8 MV/cm — roughly 4× GaN and 2× SiC. The critical figure of merit for power devices (Baliga's FOM) sits an order of magnitude above SiC. Native substrates are grown via edge-defined film-fed growth (EFG) or Czochralski, now available at 4-inch (Flosfia, Novel Crystal Technology, Tamura). Doping is achieved n-type via Si or Ge; p-type remains problematic (no shallow acceptors), constraining bipolar designs. State-of-art MOSFETs and Schottky diodes demonstrate breakdown voltages >1 kV at on-resistances competitive with SiC theoretical limits. Thermal conductivity (~10–15 W/m·K) is the primary liability.

## Cloudberry relevance

Ga₂O₃ competes directly with SiC and GaN in 650V–10 kV power switching. SiC dominates mature supply chains (Wolfspeed, onsemi, STMicro); GaN owns sub-650V high-frequency niches (Navitas, EPC). Diamond and AlN represent longer-horizon UWBG alternatives but face worse manufacturability. β-Ga₂O₃'s structural advantage is native-substrate availability at lower cost than SiC boule growth.

| Material | Eg (eV) | Breakdown field (MV/cm) | Native substrate | Maturity |
|----------|---------|------------------------|-----------------|---------|
| SiC | 3.3 | 2.5 | Yes | HVM |
| GaN | 3.4 | 3.3 | No (on Si/SiC) | HVM |
| β-Ga₂O₃ | 4.8 | ~8 | Yes | Early |

---

Ga₂O₃ sits squarely in Cloudberry's power-semiconductors thesis: a materials transition with defensible IP, substrate scarcity, and a decade-long ramp before commoditisation. Deal-flow angles include substrate suppliers, epitaxy tooling, and fabless device designers targeting EV drivetrain or grid infrastructure. GlobalFoundries' LP context matters — any Ga₂O₃ play ultimately needs fab partnership or captive process, creating an integration question worth probing at term-sheet stage.
