# Lunar Surface Power

*Kind: technology*

## Physics / mechanism

Lunar surface power addresses the fundamental energy constraint on any sustained lunar presence: the Moon's 354-hour day/night cycle, with two-week darkness periods dropping surface temperatures to –173 °C. Photovoltaic arrays (monocrystalline silicon or III-V multijunction, 28–35% efficiency in AM0 spectrum) work on the sunlit surface but require massive energy storage or geographic solution—hence strong interest in permanently shadowed region (PSR) avoidance and polar ridge siting where near-continuous illumination is achievable (~89% duty cycle at Shackleton rim). Fission surface power (FSP) is the competing baseload answer: NASA/DoE's Kilopower/KRUSTY demonstrated 10 kWe from a uranium-molybdenum fast reactor with Stirling convertors; the FSP project targets 10–40 kWe units for 2030 deployment. Transmission via high-voltage DC cable or microwave/laser beaming adds system complexity.

## Competitive landscape

| Approach | Power density | Day/night tolerance | TRL |
|---|---|---|---|
| PV + battery/RFC | Low (area-intensive) | Poor without storage | 6–7 |
| Fission surface power | High (~kWe/m³) | Full | 5–6 |
| Laser/microwave WPT | Medium | Partial (line-of-sight) | 3–4 |

## Cloudberry relevance

Solid-state nuclear batteries, regenerative fuel cells using regolith-derived water, and high-temperature thermal storage in lunar regolith are adjacent approaches. Microwave WPT competes with FSP for rover/outpost recharge architectures, drawing on phased-array and rectenna IP directly overlapping terrestrial mmWave.


Cloudberry relevance concentrates in the enabling semiconductor layer: GaN and GaAs for high-efficiency space PV and rectenna chipsets, SiC power electronics for FSP thermal conversion and HVDC distribution, and photonics for WPT pointing/tracking. GlobalFoundries' compound-semi roadmap (GaN-on-SiC, SiC PDK) maps directly onto FSP power conditioning and WPT transmitter ICs—concrete deal-flow in space-grade compound semi fabs, radiation-hardened ASIC designers, and WPT IP licensors targeting NASA Artemis supply chain.
