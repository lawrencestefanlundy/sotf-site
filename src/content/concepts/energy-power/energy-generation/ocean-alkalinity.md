# Ocean Alkalinity / Seaweed

*Kind: technology*

## Physics / mechanism

Ocean alkalinity enhancement (OAE) accelerates the ocean's natural carbon buffering chemistry. Seawater absorbs CO₂ via: CO₂ + H₂O + CO₃²⁻ → 2HCO₃⁻. Adding alkalinity (crushed silicate/carbonate minerals, electrolytically produced hydroxides) shifts equilibrium, pulling atmospheric CO₂ into stable bicarbonate. Key parameters: alkalinity addition rate (µmol/kg), pH delta (target <0.3 units above baseline), monitoring via total alkaline titration and TA/DIC ratios. Seaweed (macro/microalgae) operates differently—photosynthetic biomass uptake followed by sinking or harvest+sequestration. Current OAE pilots: 1–100 tonne CO₂/ha/yr estimates pending field validation. Frontier, Running Tide, Ebb Carbon leading commercial deployment.

## Cloudberry relevance

OAE competes directly with direct air capture (DAC, $300–600/tCO₂), BECCS, enhanced rock weathering (ERW), and kelp/seaweed sinking. ERW shares mineral feedstock logistics but targets terrestrial soils. DAC has better MRV certainty but far higher CAPEX. Seaweed sinking has contested permanence and additionality. Electrochemical OAE (Ebb, Planetary) competes with mineral-dissolution OAE (Planetary Technologies, Ebb Carbon) on energy intensity: electrolytic routes need ~1–3 MWh/tCO₂ equivalent, minerals require grinding/transport energy.

| Approach | Cost estimate ($/tCO₂) | MRV maturity |
|---|---|---|
| OAE (mineral) | 50–150 | Low |
| DAC | 300–600 | High |
| ERW | 80–200 | Medium |

---

Cloudberry relevance is indirect but real. OAE and seaweed sequestration require precision sensor arrays—optical pH/TA sensors, hyperspectral imaging, low-power distributed IoT buoys—where photonics and semiconductor process innovations matter. Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV) is the unsolved hard problem; that's a systems/instrumentation play. Potential LP-level exposure through climate-tech crossover funds. Watch for sensor companies needing GF-class photonic or MEMS fabrication hitting Series A.
