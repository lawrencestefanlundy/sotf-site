# Displays

*Kind: technology · Attio deal count: 38*

## Physics / mechanism

Displays convert electrical signals into visible photons via three dominant mechanisms: LCD (liquid-crystal modulation of a backlight), OLED (electroluminescent organic emitters, ~100–1000 nit peak, sub-millisecond response), and microLED (inorganic GaN/AlInGaP emitters transferred at die level, >1M nit achievable, >100k hour lifetime). Key parameters: pixel pitch (down to ~4 µm in lab microLED), quantum efficiency (peak ~30% EQE in production microLED), contrast ratio, and power/nit. MicroLED is the current frontier — mass transfer yield and full-colour (RGB) integration remain the unsolved manufacturing problems. MiniLED backlights (zoned LED arrays behind LCD) occupy the near-term premium tier. AR/XR waveguide displays demand <2 µm pitch emitters and >10M nit brightness to compete with ambient light.

## Competitive landscape


LCD dominates volume (smartphones, TVs) but loses share on power and form factor. OLED owns premium mobile and OLED TV. MicroLED threatens both at scale but faces cost/yield barriers. Competing modalities include e-ink (bistable, ultra-low power, reflective), LCOS/DLP (projection, high lumen, bulky), and laser scanning (LBS, used in some AR). Electroluminescent quantum-dot (QLED) is a materials route to improve colour purity within existing architectures rather than a structural replacement.

| Tech | Peak brightness | Power efficiency | Maturity |
|---|---|---|---|
| OLED | ~2k nit | ~5 lm/W | Mass production |
| MicroLED | >1M nit | ~20 lm/W | Pilot/early prod |
| LCD/MiniLED | ~4k nit | ~2 lm/W | Mass production |

## Cloudberry relevance
Display is a photonics-intensive semiconductor manufacturing problem — epitaxial growth (MOCVD), die singulation, mass transfer, and driver IC integration all intersect Cloudberry's stack. GaN-on-Si process development at fabs like GlobalFoundries creates direct deal-flow into microLED epitaxy and driver ASIC suppliers. AR waveguide optics (diffractive, metasurface) are adjacent photonics bets. Lunar Ventures' software-layer focus has less leverage here, but the picks-and-shovels angle — metrology, transfer equipment, compound semiconductor IP — fits the deep-tech thesis cleanly.
