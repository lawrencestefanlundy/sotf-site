# Additive Manufacturing

*Kind: technology · Attio deal count: 18*

## Physics / mechanism

Layer-by-layer material deposition or selective solidification controlled by digital geometry files. Core modalities: powder bed fusion (PBF-L, PBF-EB), directed energy deposition (DED), vat photopolymerisation (SLA/DLP), material jetting, and binder jetting. Key parameters: layer thickness (20–100 µm typical), build rate (10–100 cm³/hr for metals), surface roughness (Ra 5–20 µm as-built), and dimensional tolerance (±0.1–0.2 mm). SOTA metal systems (EOS M 400-4, Trumpf TruPrint) run multi-laser PBF at 400 W per beam with in-situ melt-pool monitoring. Aerospace-qualified Ti-6Al-4V and Inconel 718 are production-grade. Printed electronics and photonic waveguide structures are early-stage but advancing.

## Competitive landscape


Subtractive CNC machining dominates precision metal parts; casting/forging wins on volume cost. Key competitive axes:

| Approach | Geometric freedom | Throughput | Unit cost at volume |
|---|---|---|---|
| PBF metal AM | High | Low–medium | High |
| CNC machining | Medium | High | Low–medium |
| Die casting / forging | Low | Very high | Very low |

## Cloudberry relevance
Hybrid machines (DMG Mori, Mazak) combine DED with milling. Polymer AM competes with injection moulding only below ~10k parts. Binder jetting (Desktop Metal, ExOne) closes the cost gap for medium volumes. In photonics, two-photon polymerisation (Nanoscribe) enables sub-µm features relevant to waveguide and lens fabrication.


Cloudberry relevance sits at the materials and process-equipment layer, not the printer OEM. Investment angles: advanced feedstock materials (refractory metals, III-V powders, specialty polymers for photonic components), in-situ metrology (aligns with semiconductor process-control analogies), and printed photonic/electronic integration enabling chiplet packaging innovation. GlobalFoundries context: AM for rapid prototyping of photomask tooling fixtures and custom cooling structures in fab environments. Pipeline watch: startups combining two-photon lithography with functional materials for integrated photonics substrates.
