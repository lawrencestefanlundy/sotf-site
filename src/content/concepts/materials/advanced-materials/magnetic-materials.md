# Magnetic Materials (NdFeB, ferrite, SMC)

*Kind: material*

## Physics / mechanism

NdFeB (neodymium-iron-boron) sintered magnets are the highest-energy-density permanent magnets available: BHmax up to ~420 kJ/m³, coercivity >1 MA/m. Remanence (~1.4 T) drives torque density in motors and actuators. Ferrite (SrFe₁₂O₁₉) sits at ~40 kJ/m³—cheap, corrosion-resistant, but weak. Soft magnetic composites (SMC) are iron-powder compacts: near-isotropic flux paths, low eddy-current losses at high frequency (>1 kHz), enabling 3D flux routing impossible in laminated steel. Key parameters: BHmax, Hcj (intrinsic coercivity), Tc (Curie temp), loss coefficient. NdFeB critical constraint: Dy/Tb heavy-rare-earth content for high-temperature coercivity stability.

## Cloudberry relevance

NdFeB's primary competitive threat is HTS (high-temperature superconducting) coils for large motors—superior field strength but cryogenic overhead. Alnico offers high Tc but low coercivity. Samarium-cobalt (SmCo) competes at elevated temps (>150 °C) with better corrosion resistance but higher cost and lower BHmax. SMC competes directly with silicon-steel laminations in axial-flux and transverse-flux motor geometries.

| Material | BHmax (kJ/m³) | Max use temp (°C) | Cost driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| NdFeB sintered | ~400 | 120–180 | Nd, Dy supply chain |
| SmCo | ~250 | 300+ | Co, Sm price |
| Ferrite | ~40 | 250 | Commodity |

---

EV traction motors, heat pumps, wind generators, and defence actuators all gate on magnet supply chains and geometry innovation. Cloudberry deal-flow angles: startups doing rare-earth-reduced or RE-free magnet formulations, SMC-enabled axial-flux motor stators, and magnet recycling/hydrometallurgical recovery loops. GlobalFoundries context is indirect but photonics isolators require magneto-optic garnet films—adjacent magnetic materials IP worth tracking.
