# Security & Cyber

*Kind: market · Attio deal count: 30*

## Physics / mechanism

Security & Cyber covers protection of digital infrastructure across endpoints, networks, cloud, identity, and operational technology (OT). Market scale: ~$185B in 2024, growing ~13% CAGR toward $300B+ by 2030. Key segments: network security (Palo Alto, Fortinet), identity (CrowdStrike, Okta), OT/ICS (Claroty, Dragos), and hardware/silicon-rooted trust (Infineon, NXP, Arm TrustZone). Emerging layer: post-quantum cryptography (PQC), where NIST FIPS 203/204/205 (ML-KEM, ML-DSA, SLH-DSA) are now finalized standards. Hardware security modules (HSMs) and secure enclaves define the silicon-level trust boundary. Threat surface expanding fastest at OT/IoT edge and AI inference endpoints.

## Competitive landscape


Primary competitive vectors: perimeter vs. zero-trust architecture; software-only vs. hardware-rooted trust; incumbent platform players (Microsoft Defender, Google Mandiant) vs. point-solution specialists. Adjacent segments: trusted execution environments (TEEs), physically unclonable functions (PUFs), secure elements, and confidential computing. PQC migration is a cross-cutting pressure on all crypto-dependent products.

| Approach | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| Software-only (EDR/XDR) | Rapid deploy, updates | Bypassable below OS layer |
| Secure element / HSM | Hardware root of trust | Cost, integration friction |
| PUF-based ID | No secret storage | Side-channel exposure risk |

## Cloudberry relevance
Cloudberry angle is hardware-rooted security: silicon IP (PUF, secure enclaves, PQC accelerators) fabbed at GlobalFoundries fits the fund's foundry-linked deal thesis. GF's FDX platform and 22FDX node are relevant for low-power secure elements in IoT/edge. Lunar Ventures' enterprise software exposure surfaces complementary portfolio companies. Deal-flow priority: PQC IP vendors, secure-boot silicon, and OT security hardware needing specialty process nodes.
