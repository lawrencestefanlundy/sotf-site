## Physics / mechanism

Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems integrate mechanical elements—beams, membranes, resonators, proof masses—with electronics on silicon substrates via surface or bulk micromachining. Actuation and sensing modes: electrostatic, piezoelectric, thermal, electromagnetic. Key performance parameters: resonant frequency (kHz–GHz for RF MEMS), quality factor Q (10³–10⁶ in vacuum), minimum feature size (sub-micron in advanced nodes), and noise floor (μg/√Hz for inertial sensors). Fabrication leverages CMOS-compatible processes—DRIE, sacrificial oxide release, wafer bonding—enabling monolithic integration. State of the art: BAW/FBAR filters operating above 5 GHz for 5G front-ends; consumer IMUs at <1°/hr bias instability; MEMS mirrors with >10⁵ cycle reliability for LiDAR and photonic switching.

## Competitive landscape

Piezoelectric MEMS (PZT, AlN, LiNbO₃) competes with and complements capacitive approaches—higher coupling coefficient but more complex process integration. Solid-state alternatives increasingly threaten specific niches:

| Domain | MEMS approach | Competing approach | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| RF filtering | BAW/FBAR | SAW, LC filters | Q vs. cost vs. frequency ceiling |
| Inertial sensing | Capacitive IMU | Optical gyro, magnetometer fusion | Cost vs. precision vs. SWaP |
| Optical switching | MEMS mirrors | Liquid crystal, thermo-optic | Speed vs. insertion loss vs. power |
