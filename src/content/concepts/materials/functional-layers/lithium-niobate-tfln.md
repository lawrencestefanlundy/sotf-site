# Lithium Niobate (TFLN)

*Kind: material · Attio deal count: 0*

## Physics / mechanism

Thin-film lithium niobate (TFLN) is the incumbent inorganic electro-optic platform with Pockels coefficient ~30 pm/V — well-characterized, foundry-mature. Pivotal 2026 transitions: scaling from 4"/6" to 8" (200mm) wafers via DUV stepper lithography (vs e-beam) for HVM yield; multi-project wafer runs from foundries like SAL Switzerland, CSEM/Lightium, Quantum Computing Inc; integration with active SiPh/SiN via wafer-level bonding. Strategic tie-up: UMC + Wavetek for high-volume TFLN Chiplet platform announced early 2026. TFLN is the comparison material for any new electro-optic substrate (BTO, polymer 2PP, etc.) and the volume play for CPO modulator components.
