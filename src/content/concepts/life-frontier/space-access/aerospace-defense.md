## Physics / mechanism

Aerospace & Defense (A&D) spans military platforms, satellites, launch vehicles, avionics, and defense electronics. Scope: ~$900B global market (2024), dominated by Lockheed Martin, RTX, Northrop Grumman, BAE Systems, Airbus Defence. Key parameters: SWaP (size, weight, power), radiation hardness, MTBF >100,000 hrs, operating range −55°C to +125°C. The semiconductor content per platform is rising sharply—modern fighter jets carry 3,000+ chips; LEO satellites run custom ASICs for comms and EO/IR processing. Export control regimes (ITAR/EAR) heavily gate supply chains. GaN-on-SiC dominates radar/EW RF front-ends; SiC and GaN-on-Diamond are emerging for power electronics. Photonic sensing (LIDAR, LADAR, SWIR imaging) is becoming standard in ISR payloads.

## Competitive landscape

Commercial aerospace competes for engineering talent and fab capacity with defense; civil avionics (DO-254/DO-178C qualified) overlaps significantly. Adjacent segments: space (LEO/MEO/GEO satellites), autonomous systems (UAS, loitering munitions), and hypersonics. Key technology substitution battles:

| Domain | Incumbent | Challenger |
|---|---|---|
| RF front-end | GaN-on-SiC | GaN-on-Diamond, InP |
| Power conversion | Si IGBT | SiC MOSFET |
| Optical sensing | InGaAs APD | GeSn, SWIR SiPh |
