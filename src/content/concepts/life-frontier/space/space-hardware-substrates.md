# Space-Grade Hardware Substrates

*Cross-domain research thread connecting Cloudberry deep-tech investing to State of the Future writing on space industrialisation.*

## What it is

Materials and substrates qualified for use in space — radiation-hardened, thermal-cycle-tolerant (±150°C+), vacuum-stable, manufactured to MIL-STD or NASA-STD reliability bars. Historically a niche defence/aerospace category dominated by US incumbents. Now becoming larger because:

1. Cost-down on launch (SpaceX) opens commercial space.
2. Lunar landers + space industrialisation create demand for surface hardware.
3. Defence policy (CHIPS Act, EU sovereignty programs) funds onshoring.

## Why this matters to Cloudberry

Several Cloudberry-portfolio + pipeline materials have potential space adjacencies:

- **CVD diamond heat spreaders** (**Proud**) — outstanding thermal performance, vacuum-stable, radiation-tolerant
- **Composite ceramic substrates** (**Xtherma**) — passive thermals for AI compute *and* potentially space hardware
- **Graphene thick films** (**Deep Graphene**) — ditto
- **Photonic memory** (**Aima Technology**) — radiation tolerance better than DRAM (no charge storage)
- **NV diamond sensing** (**Diasense**) — vacuum / cryogenic compatibility

Most of these are positioned for terrestrial AI/data-centre markets first, but the space adjacency is a real second-order opportunity worth tracking.

## Adjacent themes

- **[[lunar-teleoperation]]** — operating teleoperated systems on the Moon needs lunar-grade compute + sensors.
- **EU space sovereignty** — IPCEI funds for European space-grade manufacturing.
